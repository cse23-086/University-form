Overview

The University of Botswana (UB) is the first institution of higher education in Botswana, established in 1982. Located in the capital city of Gaborone, UB has grown to become the nation's leading educational institution, with additional campuses in Francistown and Maun.

With a student population of over 15,000 and a diverse academic staff drawn from across the globe, UB offers a comprehensive range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across seven faculties. The university is committed to academic excellence, research that addresses national and global challenges, and community engagement.

Mission: To improve economic and social conditions for the nation while advancing itself as a distinctively African university with a regional and international outlook.

UB consistently ranks among the top universities in Africa and has established partnerships with leading institutions worldwide, offering students opportunities for international exchange and collaborative research projects.

History & Milestones

The University of Botswana has a rich history of academic excellence and national development.

1982

University of Botswana established as an independent national university

1990

Introduction of postgraduate programs, beginning with Master's degrees

2001

Launch of the Faculty of Medicine, addressing critical healthcare needs

2008

Opening of the UB Science and Technology Park to foster innovation

2015

Ranked among top 10 universities in Africa by Times Higher Education

2020

Admissions

The University of Botswana welcomes applications from qualified students across Botswana and internationally. Our admissions process is designed to identify talented individuals who will thrive in our academic community.

Key Admission Dates:
- Undergraduate Applications: January 1 - March 31
- Postgraduate Applications: Rolling admissions (varies by program)
- Semester Begins: August (Main intake), January (Limited programs)

Entry Requirements

Admission requirements vary by program, but generally include:

  • BGCSE or equivalent with credits in relevant subjects
  • Minimum entry points as specified for each program
  • Additional requirements for specific programs (e.g., portfolio for design, interview for medicine)
  • English language proficiency for international students

How to Apply

Applications are submitted online through the UB admissions portal. Required documents typically include:

  • Certified copies of academic certificates
  • National ID or passport copy
  • Application fee payment receipt
  • Program-specific supporting documents
