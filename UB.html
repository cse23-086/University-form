Overview

The University of Botswana (UB) is the first institution of higher education in Botswana, established in 1982. Located in the capital city of Gaborone, UB has grown to become the nation's leading educational institution, with additional campuses in Francistown and Maun.

With a student population of over 15,000 and a diverse academic staff drawn from across the globe, UB offers a comprehensive range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across seven faculties. The university is committed to academic excellence, research that addresses national and global challenges, and community engagement.

Mission: To improve economic and social conditions for the nation while advancing itself as a distinctively African university with a regional and international outlook.

UB consistently ranks among the top universities in Africa and has established partnerships with leading institutions worldwide, offering students opportunities for international exchange and collaborative research projects.
